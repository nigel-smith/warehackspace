About Nigel

Nigel's story

I've been taking things apart since I was old enough to hold a screwdriver. By 1990, aged eleven, I'd got my hands on a computer, and built my own not long after. I ended up helping look after my school's network as it grew.

That same instinct carried me into twenty-five years as an electrician and gas engineer: rewiring, refitting and extending properties, first subcontracting to my dad's building firm, then running things together for his last decade before he retired. When he stepped back, I went back to computing and retrained as a network engineer. I have a degree in Cybernetics and I'm partway through a Master's in Computer Science.

Before Ware, I joined BradLUG, a Linux user group in Bradford, in September 2008, a few months after it started as an informal meet-up. I wasn't there for the very first session, but I was part of turning it from something loose into something properly run, and it's still going well over a decade later, I'm still technically an admin. I was its treasurer in the early years, on the back of time as secretary and then treasurer of a school PTA charity, so I've sat on the unglamorous but necessary side of keeping a community group alive as well as the fun side.

I moved to Ware in 2018. Music's been a constant for about 35 years: I studied music technology at college, build my own electric guitars, once hand-wired a car from a bare metal shell, and now play in a band and run a small guitar-teaching and studio business locally.

Ware Hackspace comes out of all of that: wanting a place in town to build things with other people, whether that's a soldering iron, a set of spanners, or a coil of guitar wire in hand.
