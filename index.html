Who this is for

Built for everyone in Ware

This isn't just for people who already call themselves "techy." A hackspace works best with a real mix of people: complete beginners curious about 3D printing, retired engineers who fancy a project, students learning to code, parents looking for something to do with the kids on a Saturday, and everyone in between. No particular background or experience is assumed, and there's no such thing as a silly question.

We're new — there's no fixed night or venue yet, and we're still finding our feet — but the idea is straightforward and the door's open. If it sounds like your kind of thing, the best way to help it happen is to say so.

The basics

What's a hackspace?

A hackspace (sometimes called a makerspace) is a shared, community-run space where people interested in technology and making can meet, share tools, swap skills, and work on their own or group projects. They're almost always run not-for-profit, and membership is usually open to anyone — no qualifications required, just curiosity.

Most hackspaces run on a mix of regular open sessions, where people simply turn up and work on whatever they're into, and occasional workshops or talks where someone shows everyone else how to do something. Here's the kind of thing that tends to come up:

  • Electronics & soldering

    Build and fix circuits, learn to solder, take old electronics apart to see how they work.

  • 3D printing & making

    Design and print parts, prototype ideas, and turn projects from screen into object.

  • Computing & repairs

    Build, upgrade and fix computers, play with boards like the Raspberry Pi, and pick up new skills.

  • Skill-shares & talks

    Short, informal sessions where someone shows everyone else something useful — no pressure, no exam at the end.

Computing, focused on Linux & open source

What's Ware LUG?

A Linux User Group, or LUG, is an informal community for people who use — or want to learn — Linux and other free and open-source software. LUGs have been running since the 1990s, and almost all of them work the same simple way: people who know things help people who want to learn, in person, on a regular night.

As far as I can find, there isn't currently a LUG covering this part of Hertfordshire, so it's part of the plan from day one rather than something bolted on later. The hope is a separate, regular Ware LUG night running alongside the wider hackspace — same community, different focus.

  • Install fests — bring a laptop and get help installing or dual-booting Linux.
  • Talks & show-and-tell on open-source projects, tools, and interesting bits of tech.
  • Help for complete beginners — no "just use the terminal" gatekeeping.
  • A social side — plenty of LUGs end up being as much about the chat afterwards as the meeting itself.

Founding roadmap

What needs to happen, in order

  1. Done — Get something online so people can find us and get in touch
  2. Not yet — Meet up informally, somewhere with zero overhead like a pub, and see who else is interested
  3. Not yet — Settle into a regular night, and a separate one for Ware LUG
  4. Not yet — Grow enough to need real space, not just a corner of a pub
  5. Not yet — Find a proper home, somewhere we can leave tools and machinery between sessions
  6. Not yet — Start sharing that home with other local groups doing similar things
